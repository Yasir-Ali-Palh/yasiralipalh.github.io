The P-39 - A Weapon in Skilled Hands

The P-39 was a fighter aircraft which served in the second world war. It was quite a distinctive aircraft, in that unlike its contemporaries it had car-like doors instead of a sliding canopy and its engine was installed behind the cockpit instead of infront of it. It served with US in the Pacific theatre and it was also land-leased to the USSR. The US pilots bad-mouthed it because it was hard to gain an advantage over the Japanese Zero with it, only a select few distinguished pilots were able to succeed in it. The Soviets on the other hand had a much better time with it. It mainly served in the Caucasus, where the fighting took place mostly at a lower altitude. The Soviets also removed two of its machine-guns in order to save weight. Both German and Soviet pilots agreed that it was a formidable fighter in the hands of a skilled pilot. A German pilot even said that it was the best Soviet fighter in service. Eventually, as the war drew on, it was replaced by more advanced fighters.

Following are some of the P-39's Specifications:

  • Maximum speed: 389 mph (626 km/h, 338 kn)
  • Range: 525 mi (845 km, 456 nmi) on internal fuel
  • Service ceiling: 35,000 ft (11,000 m)
  • Guns:
    • 1 x 37 mm M4 cannon firing through the propeller hub
    • 2 x .50 caliber synchronized Browning M2 machine guns, nose-mounted
    • 2 x .50 caliber Browning M2 machine guns one each wing
